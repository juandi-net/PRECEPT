# quality-baseline

**Scope:** org-wide
**Source:** Generated from Precepts at onboarding
**Tags:** quality, standards, baseline

## Guidance

Every piece of work ROOKIE produces — code, copy, design, hardware decisions, partner communications — must meet a standard that reflects the seriousness of what we're building. Players are trusting us with their effort. We don't ship sloppy.

**Baseline standards for all output:**

### 1. Does it work as intended?

If it's code, it runs without critical bugs in the target environment. If it's hardware, it performs the function it's designed for. If it's a message, it communicates the intended idea clearly. "Good enough to test" is fine for internal prototypes. "Good enough to ship" means it works reliably for the person using it.

### 2. Is it honest?

No overpromising. No vague claims. No hiding limitations behind jargon. If the sensor prototype is taped to a ball, we call it a prototype. If the app can classify dribbles but not crossovers yet, we say that. ROOKIE's credibility is built on telling the truth, especially when the truth is "we're early."

### 3. Does it respect the player's time and intelligence?

If it's a user-facing feature, the player should be able to understand what it does and why it matters without a manual. If it's instructions, they should be clear enough that someone can follow them without guessing. If it's a request for data or permission, the terms should be transparent. We don't trick people into things.

### 4. Is it aligned with ROOKIE's moral and mission constraints?

Before shipping anything public-facing, ask: Does this violate Biblical standards (gambling mechanics, sexualized content, anything that promotes lust, adultery, or dishonesty)? Does this undermine player ownership of their data? Does this prioritize profit over impact in a way that conflicts with the mission? If yes to any, it doesn't ship.

### 5. Can it survive contact with the real world?

ROOKIE is building for a global audience — players in different countries, with different devices, different levels of technical literacy, different internet speeds. If your work assumes everyone has an iPhone 15 and fiber internet, revise it. Aim for resilient, not perfect.

**When quality is negotiable:**

Speed matters when we're learning. If you're testing an idea internally (e.g., "can the BMI270 detect a dribble?"), ship fast and iterate. But once it touches a player, a partner, or the public, the baseline applies.

**When in doubt:**

Ask yourself: Would I be proud to show this to the friend from Burundi who inspired ROOKIE? If the answer is no, keep working.
