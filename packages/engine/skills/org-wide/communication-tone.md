# communication-tone

**Scope:** org-wide
**Source:** Generated from Precepts at onboarding
**Tags:** communication, writing, tone

## Guidance

ROOKIE's voice is grounded, direct, and human. We speak to players, partners, and the public with clarity and conviction — no hype, no fluff, no performative tech-speak.

**Core principles:**

- **Speak to the player, not the investor.** Our primary audience is the athlete who shows up every day. Write for them first. If a message works for a player in Burundi and a scout in Los Angeles, it's probably right.

- **Use active voice and concrete language.** "The ball captures your shot data" beats "Performance metrics are recorded via sensor integration." If you can't explain it to someone at the court, rewrite it.

- **Lead with the human truth, not the technology.** The story is always about effort, ownership, and what the player gets — not about nRF chips or ML models. Technology is infrastructure. The player's work is the headline.

- **Avoid sports clichés and motivational platitudes.** No "grind," "hustle culture," or "be legendary." ROOKIE respects the work by being honest about it. The ball doesn't make you great — it proves you already are.

- **Be matter-of-fact about the big vision.** ROOKIE is building a protocol for human effort. That's true. Say it plainly when relevant, but don't oversell. Let the idea carry its own weight.

- **Default to "you" and "your."** The player owns their data, their effort, their tokens. Reinforce that ownership in every message.

**Formality:** Conversational but not casual. Professional but not corporate. Think: a coach who respects you enough to tell the truth.

**Word choice:** Favor simple, strong nouns and verbs. "Capture" over "leverage." "Own" over "control." "Proof" over "validation."

**When in doubt:** Read it aloud. If it sounds like a press release, start over.
