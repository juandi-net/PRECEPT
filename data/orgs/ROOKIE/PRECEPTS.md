# Precepts

Generated from onboarding session on 2026-03-02.

## Identity

ROOKIE is a sports technology company building a sensor-equipped basketball that captures real player performance data (shot accuracy, intensity, movement patterns) and packages it into data-backed tokens. The core analogy: Bitcoin converts computer energy into tokens, ROOKIE converts human energy into tokens — the ball is the mining device. The company sits at the intersection of sports hardware, async global competition, and data-as-value philosophy. Solo founder: Juandi. Deeper mission: using sport and competition as a vehicle to connect men globally, build accountability and dedication, and combat loneliness. The ball and tokens are the mechanism — the real purpose is giving men a reason to show up consistently for something that's good for them, and through that, find belonging. The user experience delivers three things: anticipation (something to look forward to), accountability (a team to show up for), and proof of effort (a tangible token backed by real performance data — not a badge, but a data-rich receipt of work done). Origin story: Juandi's friend from Burundi was training twice daily at 18, fully committed to making the NBA. He had to stop and drive DoorDash 15 hours a day to provide for his family back home. The dream died not because of lack of effort but because there was no way to convert that effort into something tangible. ROOKIE exists because those hours, that passion, that dedication has real value — and there should be a system that captures and recognizes it. The value realization model: ROOKIE structures and organizes raw performance data so the player owns it and can trade it to parties who derive value from it — scouts, coaches, analytics companies, brands. At scale, the data buyer market extends well beyond sports: robotics companies, ML/LLM companies training models, and others who need high-fidelity human movement data. The basketball is a natural, high-frequency capture device because people want to use it — no lab required. The player isn't auditioning; they're producing. ROOKIE doesn't invent the value, it reveals it and gives the player control over it. This fundamentally shifts the power dynamic in grassroots sports. Long-term vision: ROOKIE is the beginning of a universal protocol for converting human effort into tradeable data tokens — starting with basketball, expanding to all sports, and eventually any trackable human activity (dancing, singing, etc.). In a future where data becomes the new store of value, ROOKIE gives individuals ownership and monetization of the data their effort produces. The player never needs to understand the big picture — they just need to love their sport. ROOKIE handles the infrastructure.

## Product / Service *(hypothesis)*

Current product state: 50 branded basketballs (logo only, no sensor). No working hardware prototype. No app. The product as envisioned is a sensor-equipped basketball connected to an app that captures performance data, but nothing functional exists yet. Immediate plan: purchase off-the-shelf sensors (nRF52840 DK + BMI270 breakout — under $100 total), tape them to the ball, build a basic app to harvest raw sensor data, and feed it into an LLM to attempt movement classification — specifically differentiating dribbles, shots, and other basketball actions. This is the fundamental proof of concept: can the ball tell us what the player is doing? Founder has high confidence in feasibility — other companies (including Wilson) have successfully embedded sensors in basketballs and classified shots in the past 5-7 years. Modern algorithms are significantly more advanced, which should make classification easier and richer (not just made/missed shots, but energy levels in dribbles, passes, etc.).

## Competitive Landscape

Several companies have built sensor-equipped basketballs in the past 5-7 years (including Wilson). However, founder does not view them as competitors. Key distinction: those companies positioned themselves as training tools to help players improve their game. ROOKIE is fundamentally different — it's not a training tool, it's a connection and value-capture ecosystem. The ball is a mining device, not a gadget. Same hardware concept, completely different purpose and market positioning. Known prior players: Wilson X (sensor in ball — discontinued), 94Fifty (sensor in ball — believed discontinued), Homecourt (camera-based tracking, no sensor in ball — poor UX in founder's view, no sensor). Most or all have faded out, suggesting the training-tool use case alone wasn't sticky enough to sustain, even with major brand backing. This validates the hardware feasibility while pointing to the need for a fundamentally different value proposition — which is exactly what ROOKIE offers.

## Data Policy

Core principle: The player owns their data. ROOKIE structures, packages, and makes performance data tradeable, but the player decides what to do with it — sell, trade, hold, invest. ROOKIE is the infrastructure, not the gatekeeper. Data buyers can be anyone who finds value in it — sports companies, robotics companies, AI/ML companies. Transactions happen on the player's terms. Open question: Whether ROOKIE could sell aggregated/anonymized data at scale — founder's filter is whether it would devalue individual player data. If yes, it's off the table. If it can be done without hurting the player and it grows the company, it's worth exploring. Marked for future research when real data exists at scale.
